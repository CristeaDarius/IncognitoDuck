Ostrów Tumski, often referred to as Cathedral Island, is a historically and culturally significant district in Opole, Poland. This picturesque island is located on the Odra River and has been a focal point since the early days of Polish Christianity, making it one of the oldest districts in the city. Archaeological findings suggest that settlement on Ostrów Tumski dates back to the 9th century, which aligns with the spread of Christianity in the region. The island has been home to several important buildings over the centuries, with the Opole Cathedral being a standout structure, reflective of various architectural styles, including Romanesque and Gothic elements. The cathedral's construction began in the 10th century, and it has undergone several renovations and restorations, particularly after damages in the Second World War.
